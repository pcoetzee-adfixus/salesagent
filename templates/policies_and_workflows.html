Brand Manifest Policy

Control when advertisers must provide brand information (brand_manifest) when discovering products or creating campaigns.

About these options

🔐 Require Authentication (Recommended for most publishers)

  • Advertisers must authenticate to see products
  • brand_manifest is optional
  • Pricing shown to authenticated users
  • Best for: Standard B2B sales, testing environments

🔒 Require Authentication + Brand Manifest (Strictest)

  • Advertisers must authenticate AND provide brand context
  • brand_manifest is required
  • Pricing shown only with brand context
  • Best for: Bespoke products requiring brand-specific customization

🌐 Public (Most open)

  • No authentication required
  • brand_manifest optional
  • Pricing hidden (generic catalog only)
  • Best for: Public product browsing, lead generation
{% endif %}

Naming Conventions

Configure how orders and line items are named. Works across all ad servers (GAM, Mock).

Adapter Support: ✅ Google Ad Manager    ✅ Mock Adapter
Show available variables
Available variables:
  • {campaign_name} - Campaign name (optional - may be empty)
  • {promoted_offering} - What's being advertised (always present)
  • {media_buy_id} - Media buy identifier (always present)
  • {auto_name} - 🤖 AI-generated name from full context (requires Gemini API key)
  • {date_range} - Flight dates (e.g., "Oct 7-14, 2025")
  • {month_year} - Month and year (e.g., "Oct 2025")
  • {package_count} - Number of packages in order
Fallback syntax: {var1|var2} uses var1 if present, otherwise var2
Example: {campaign_name|promoted_offering} tries campaign_name first, falls back to promoted_offering
AI Naming: {auto_name} generates smart, concise names (~500ms). Falls back to promoted_offering if Gemini unavailable.
Show available variables
Available variables:
  • {product_name} - Product/inventory name (e.g., "Display 300x250")
  • {order_name} - Parent order name (provides campaign context)
  • {package_index} - Package position number (1, 2, 3...)
Tip: Including {order_name} helps identify campaigns in line-item reports

Example 1: Simple Three-State Review 
APPROVE if:
✓ Creative clearly matches promoted offering

✓ Appropriate for general audiences

REQUIRE HUMAN APPROVAL if:
⚠️ Unsure about promoted offering match

Example 2: Brand Safety Focus 
APPROVE if all conditions met:
- Clearly promotes the advertised product
- No offensive, violent, or inappropriate content
- No false or misleading claims
- Professional image quality

REQUIRE HUMAN APPROVAL if:
- Contains claims that need fact-checking
- Uses controversial themes or messaging
- Quality is acceptable but not ideal
- Uncertain about brand guideline compliance

REJECT if any condition met:
- Violates advertising standards
- Contains competitor branding
- Promotes wrong or unrelated products
- Unprofessional or low quality
Example 3: Detailed Guidelines 
Review each creative and respond with one of three actions:

APPROVE - Creative is ready to go live:
✓ Promoted offering matches creative content
✓ Professional quality (clear images, readable text)
✓ Appropriate for all audiences
✓ No misleading claims or fine print issues
✓ Brand/product clearly visible
✓ Meets industry advertising standards

REQUIRE HUMAN APPROVAL - Needs manual review:
⚠️ Uncertain about promoted offering alignment
⚠️ Questionable claims that may need verification
⚠️ Sensitive topics or mature themes
⚠️ Unusual format or creative approach
⚠️ Borderline quality or professionalism
⚠️ Potential legal or compliance concerns

REJECT - Creative cannot be approved:
✗ Wrong product/service advertised
✗ Poor quality or unprofessional appearance
✗ Inappropriate, offensive, or explicit content

Rank products by relevance to the brief, prioritizing:
1. Target audience match
2. Budget efficiency (lower CPM for similar reach)
3. Format compatibility with campaign goals
Return up to 10 products, sorted by overall fit.

Rank products considering:
- Brand safety alignment with the advertiser
